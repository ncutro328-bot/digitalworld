Meditation Routine for Beginners

Step 1: Preparation
  • Find a quiet and comfortable space: This could be a chair, bed, or floor.
  • Set a timer: Start with 5–10 minutes for beginners.
  • Close your eyes: If you prefer, you can keep them open and focus on a fixed point.
Step 2: Focus on Your Breath
  • Bring your attention to your breath: Notice the sensation of inhaling and exhaling.
  • Observe your breath without judgment: Simply follow the rise and fall of your chest or abdomen.
  • If your mind wanders, gently bring it back to your breath: This is normal, so don't get discouraged.
Step 3: Optional Techniques
  • Body scan: Gradually scan your body from head to toe, noticing any sensations or tension.
  • Mantra: Repeat a calming word or phrase, such as "om" or "peace."
  • Guided meditation: Use a recorded or live meditation guide to lead you through the process.
Step 4: Ending the Meditation
  • Take a few deep breaths: Allow your body to settle.
  • Open your eyes slowly: If you closed them earlier.
  • Take a moment to reflect: Notice how you feel and any insights gained.
Tips for Consistency
  • Start small: Gradually increase your meditation time as you become more comfortable.
  • Meditate regularly: Aim for daily or weekly sessions.
  • Experiment with different techniques: Find what works best for you.
  • Be patient: Meditation is a skill that takes time to develop.
  • Don't give up: If you miss a session, don't get discouraged. Just get back on track when you can.
