In the Moment

  1. Pause and Breathe
    • Try a quick grounding breath: inhale slowly through your nose for 4 seconds, hold for 2, exhale through your mouth for 6.
    • This physically signals your nervous system to slow down.
  2. Notice, Don’t Judge
    • Instead of thinking “I shouldn’t feel this way,” tell yourself “I’m feeling anxious right now, and that’s okay.”
    • Labeling the feeling gives your brain some distance from it.
  3. Ground Yourself in the Present
    • Look around and name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.
    • It anchors you in your senses instead of your thoughts.
  4. Relax Your Body
    • Check your shoulders, jaw, and hands — tension collects there.
    • Release them consciously.

Over Time

  1. Build Calm into Routine
    • Short daily moments — walking, journaling, stretching — help your body practice calm before stress hits.
    • Even 5–10 minutes a day matters.
  2. Simplify Inputs
    • Too many notifications, voices, or tasks amplify anxiety.
    • Reduce what’s unnecessary — fewer tabs, fewer alerts, fewer “shoulds.”
  3. Rest and Nourish
    • Sleep, water, and balanced meals aren’t just health clichés — they stabilize mood and focus.
  4. Reframe Stress
    • Ask: “What’s in my control right now?”
    • Focusing on small, concrete actions makes big problems less overwhelming.
